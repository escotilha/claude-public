### 1. The "Período de Teste" — 2026 structure

LC 214/2025, **Art. 348** (Disposições Transitórias) establishes a mandatory **"período de teste"** for 2026 covering both CBS (Contribuição sobre Bens e Serviços) and IBS (Imposto sobre Bens e Serviços) at **residual/symbolic rates**:

- **CBS test rate (2026):** 0,1% over the same tax base that triggers PIS/COFINS today. This is explicitly framed as a "cobrança simbólica" — actual CBS rates only escalate post-2027.
- **IBS test rate (2026):** 0,1% (to be distributed proportionally by State/Municipality reference ratios). This runs alongside ICMS and ISS, which remain **fully in force at their existing rates** throughout 2026.

The dual-run in 2026 is therefore **asymmetric**: old taxes (PIS, COFINS, ICMS, ISS) continue at 100% of current rates; CBS and IBS appear at 0.1% each — a compliance dry run, not a real revenue-replacement event.

### 2. Which taxes run in parallel and on what base?

| Tax | 2026 Status | Rate | Calculating Base |
|---|---|---|---|
| PIS | **Full force** | 0.65% (cumulativo) / 1.65% (não-cumulativo) | Faturamento/receita bruta (unchanged) |
| COFINS | **Full force** | 3% (cumulativo) / 7.6% (não-cumulativo) | Faturamento/receita bruta (unchanged) |
| ICMS | **Full force** | State-specific (avg ~12-18%) | Circulação de mercadorias |
| ISS | **Full force** | Municipal (2%-5%) | Prestação de serviços |
| CBS | **Test run** | **0.1%** | Same base as PIS/COFINS |
| IBS | **Test run** | **0.1%** | Same base as ICMS+ISS (combined operation) |

Critically, LC 214 provides an **offset mechanism** for CBS and IBS test-period amounts: taxpayers can credit the 0.1% CBS payment against PIS/COFINS due in the same competência (Art. 349, §2º). Likewise, IBS test-period payments offset against ICMS or ISS at the taxpayer's election — preventing true double-collection at the symbolic rates.

### 3. Compliance obligations generated by the dual-run

Even at 0.1%, LC 214 creates **real filing obligations** beginning January 2026:

- Taxpayers must issue **NF-e/NFS-e** with CBS and IBS fields populated (the NFe layout will require CBS and IBS as separate line items, requiring ERP/fiscal-engine changes).
- A new **declaração de apuração** (DCTF-Web variant or successor) must report CBS and IBS alongside PIS/COFINS, ICMS (via SPED), and ISS.
- The **Comitê Gestor do IBS** (established by LC 214, Art. 150+) begins data collection in 2026 for its eventual revenue-distribution role among States and Municipalities — so IBS apuração data has real institutional downstream use even at 0.1%.

The shadow-ledger engineering challenge for a fiscal SaaS like Contably is therefore: **two additional tax calculations per fiscal event, with new credit/offset logic, on every nota fiscal issued from January 2026**.

---

### 4. Phase-out timeline (context for scoping)

The dual-run intensifies after 2026:

- **2027:** CBS rises to full rate (estimated ~8.8% consolidated, replacing PIS/COFINS). PIS/COFINS extinguished.
- **2029–2032:** IBS rate ramps up annually; ICMS and ISS rates correspondingly reduced each year (State/Municipal loss-compensation mechanism via Fundo de Compensação).
- **2033:** ICMS and ISS fully extinguished. IBS at full rate.

The 2026 test period is explicitly the **only year** where all six taxes (PIS, COFINS, ICMS, ISS, CBS, IBS) coexist simultaneously — making it the highest-complexity fiscal year of the entire transition.

---

### 5. Sevilha-relevant complexity drivers

For a contabilidade/fiscal engine product targeting Brazilian SMEs:

- The **offset logic** (CBS credit vs. PIS/COFINS; IBS credit vs. ICMS/ISS) requires per-competência reconciliation — not just parallel calculation.
- **NFe layout changes** are mandatory infrastructure: the schema update for CBS/IBS fields will be enforced by SEFAZ as a hard validation gate.
- **Simples Nacional carve-out**: LC 214 Art. 16 exempts Simples Nacional optants from CBS/IBS in 2026 (they remain under the DAS unified regime). A fiscal engine must check regime before applying dual-run logic — this is a non-trivial branch.
- **Service-sector complexity**: ISS has ~5,500 municipal tables; IBS in 2026 runs on a "reference alíquota" rather than actual municipal rates — meaning IBS base-rate calculation in the test period uses a national reference, not the actual local ISS rate the taxpayer pays.
